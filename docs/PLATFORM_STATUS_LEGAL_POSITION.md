# Platform Status and Legal Position

## Purpose

This document defines the platform’s intended legal and operational posture for all product, design, copywriting, moderation, and agent behavior.

The platform is a **self-guided challenge publishing and discovery platform**. It is not an event organizer, race promoter, sanctioning body, land manager, guide service, or safety authority.

Users may create and publish outdoor challenges, routes, stages, checkpoints, or missions. Other users may independently choose to attempt those challenges on their own time, at their own risk, and subject to all applicable laws, land-use rules, trail rules, and permit requirements.

## Core Position

The platform provides tools for publishing and discovering user-created challenge information.

The platform does **not** organize, host, supervise, sanction, permit, insure, staff, mark, marshal, or control any challenge.

A challenge on the platform should be treated as **user-generated informational content**, not as a platform-run event.

## Platform Is Not the Organizer

The platform must never represent that it is organizing or operating a challenge.

The platform does not:

- Select or approve routes as safe
- Guarantee that a route is legal for bikes or public access
- Obtain permits for user-created challenges
- Provide insurance for challenge creators or participants
- Provide medical support, marshals, signage, aid stations, traffic control, or sweep riders
- Control start times, attendance, group size, or participant conduct
- Require participants to appear at a specific place and time
- Grant exclusive use of trails, parks, roads, or public land
- Create official race results
- Award prize money based on performance

## Challenge Creator Responsibility

Challenge creators are responsible for the challenges they publish.

Before publishing, the creator should confirm:

- The challenge is self-guided and independently attempted.
- The challenge is not being promoted as a race or organized event.
- The creator has verified that the route is legally accessible and open to bikes.
- The creator has reviewed applicable trail rules, land-use restrictions, seasonal closures, fire closures, and park rules.
- The creator is responsible for any permit, insurance, or land-manager approval required by their own conduct.
- The creator will not encourage trespassing, illegal trail use, unsafe riding, trail damage, night riding where prohibited, speed violations, or conflicts with hikers/equestrians.
- The creator will not coordinate a mass start, timed race, staffed course, or organized gathering through the platform unless they independently obtain all required approvals outside the platform.

## Participant Responsibility

Participants are responsible for their own decision to attempt a challenge.

Before joining or attempting, participants should acknowledge:

- Challenges are self-guided and unsupervised.
- The platform does not verify safety, legality, conditions, closures, difficulty, hazards, or suitability.
- Outdoor riding involves inherent risks including injury, death, weather, wildlife, falls, collisions, navigation errors, mechanical failure, and trail-condition changes.
- Participants must obey all trail rules, posted signs, speed limits, closures, and land-manager instructions.
- Participants must yield appropriately to hikers, equestrians, and other users.
- Participants must ride within their ability and use appropriate equipment.
- Participants are responsible for navigation, hydration, repairs, emergency planning, and communications.

## First Amendment Alignment

The platform should be designed and described primarily as a tool for user-generated speech and information sharing: routes, descriptions, notes, photos, difficulty estimates, safety warnings, and completion stories.

This supports a First Amendment-aligned posture because the platform is facilitating publication, discovery, moderation, and organization of expressive and informational content.

However, the agent must never claim that the First Amendment eliminates liability or makes the platform risk-free.

The First Amendment does not convert unlawful conduct into protected speech, does not remove permit requirements, and does not protect the platform if it materially participates in organizing, directing, supervising, or facilitating an unlawful or unsafe activity.

The platform’s safest posture is:

> We publish and moderate user-generated challenge information. We do not organize or operate physical events.

## Section 230 Alignment

The platform should also be designed with a user-generated-content model in mind.

Where challenge details, routes, descriptions, comments, photos, warnings, and updates are created by users, the platform should preserve a clear distinction between:

- **User-created content**, for which the creator is responsible
- **Platform-created content**, for which the platform may have more direct responsibility

The agent must not assume Section 230 applies to every claim or situation. Section 230 may help protect a platform from being treated as the publisher or speaker of third-party content, but it generally does not protect the platform’s own content, offline conduct, contractual promises, product-design choices, or direct participation in an activity.

## Product Rules for Risk Reduction

The product should avoid features that make the platform look like an event organizer.

Prefer:

- Publish challenge
- Discover challenge
- Save route
- Attempt challenge independently
- Mark completed
- Upload proof
- Earn badge
- Share completion
- Report issue
- Flag unsafe or illegal challenge

Avoid or review carefully:

- Event registration
- Start waves
- Race clock
- Mandatory start time
- Official leaderboard based on fastest time
- Podium placement
- Prize distribution
- Mass-start coordination
- Attendance caps controlled by platform
- Permitting claims
- Insurance claims
- Course marshal assignment
- Medical support claims
- “Officially approved” route language

## Leaderboards and Timing

Leaderboards, fastest-time rankings, and official timing can make a challenge look more like a race.

If timing is used, prefer safer framing:

- Personal progress tracking
- Completion verification
- Personal best
- Non-official community stats
- No prizes based on time
- No platform-certified race results
- No mass start
- No required start window

## Moderation Rules

The platform should remove, hide, or require edits to challenges that appear to involve:

- Illegal trail access
- Trespassing
- Closed trails or restricted land
- E-bike use where prohibited
- Unsafe public-road routing without appropriate context
- Encouragement to ignore speed limits, trail rules, or closures
- Mass-start coordination without permits
- Prize-based racing
- Harassment of other trail users
- Trail damage, unauthorized trail building, or cutting switchbacks
- Instructions to evade land managers or law enforcement

## Agent Behavior Rules

The agent must:

- Describe the platform as a self-guided challenge platform.
- Avoid calling user-created challenges “races” or “events.”
- Avoid saying the platform takes “no risk.”
- Use “risk reduction,” “risk allocation,” and “not the organizer” instead.
- Recommend attorney review for Terms, waiver flow, minors, insurance, land-use compliance, and California-specific risk.
- Make clear that creators and participants have independent responsibilities.
- Flag features that could make the platform look like a race organizer.

The agent must not:

- Promise immunity.
- Promise that the First Amendment fully protects the platform.
- Promise that Section 230 fully protects the platform.
- Tell creators they do not need permits.
- Tell participants that a route is safe or legal unless verified by an appropriate authority.
- Encourage public-land use that conflicts with park rules or trail restrictions.

## Suggested Platform Copy

### Short Positioning

Self-guided MTB challenges across LA’s canyons, coast, and mountains.

### Platform Disclaimer

Challenges on this platform are user-created, self-guided routes and missions. They are not platform-organized races, supervised events, or sanctioned competitions. Riders choose whether to attempt a challenge independently and at their own risk. Challenge creators and riders are responsible for following all trail rules, land-use restrictions, closures, permit requirements, and local laws.

### Creator Confirmation

By publishing this challenge, I confirm that I am responsible for the challenge content I create. I understand this platform does not organize, permit, insure, supervise, or operate this challenge. I have checked that the route is appropriate for public use and will not promote this challenge as a race, mass start, or organized event unless I independently obtain all required permissions, permits, and insurance.

### Participant Confirmation

I understand this is a self-guided, user-created challenge. It is not a race, supervised event, or guided activity. I am responsible for my own safety, equipment, route choice, trail etiquette, legal compliance, and decision to participate.

## Final Principle

The platform should always preserve the distinction between **publishing information about self-guided challenges** and **organizing physical events**.

That distinction is central to the product, the brand, the legal posture, and the agent’s reasoning.
